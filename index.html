Somaliland

The Republic of Somaliland is a de facto sovereign state which is considered by most countries to be part of Somalia. It lies in eastern Africa on the Gulf of Aden and has a population of 5.7 million on 177,000 kmÂ². With a total GDP of under 5 billion USD and a GDP per capita of 310 USD in 2020, the country and its inhabitants are very poor. In fact, it is one of the poorest regions in the world.


In recent years Somaliland has faced many crises, but the most urgent one is global warming which has led to droughts, poor living conditions and rural flight. Here you can find information about the global issue of climate change, its local consequences in Somaliland and the way in which we can help Somaliland overcome its most difficult challenge.

Rural flight

Because of the lack of water and the resulting spare vegetation, rural areas are faced with the problem of dying livestock and since most of the soil doesn't allow for any farming, hunger and starvation are the consequences. Trying to escape those conditions, many villagers and farmers abandon their homes and migrate to the cities. Hawo Mohamed is one of those migrants. Her story illustrates the terrible situation and shows the despair of the locals.

Read more

Nothing but droughts

Arid weather is a daily reality for the people of Somaliland, but the local differences are enormous. While in the south-west of the region the annual rainfall is comparable to the rainfall in cities like Berlin or Kiel, the rainfall in the east is almost none existent. On top of that, droughts are further worsening the situations in the entire region and are responsible for most of the many problems in the region.

Trucking water through the desert

Our emergency relief programm involves extracing drinking water from seawater through large desalination plants at the coast and transporting it to the cities and remote villages by truck. The project is still in it's initial phase but it has already achieved very promising results.

Tearfund

Tearfunds main goal in Somaliland is to strengthen village communities and increase their resilience so that people can counter their local problems and crises through knowledge, planning, preparation, and collaboration. They will achieve this through projects in water and agriculture, health promotion, and economic development.

act!onaid

In Somaliland act!onaid is helping people fight for the rights that they are denied. Simple things, like the right to eat. The right to stay on their land. To an education. To have a say in the decisions that shape their lives.

CARE

CARE has been providing emergency relief and lifesaving assistance to Somaliland since 1981. Their main program activities since then have included projects in water and sanitation, sustainable pastoralist activities, small-scale enterprise development, primary school education, teacher training, adult literacy and vocational training.
